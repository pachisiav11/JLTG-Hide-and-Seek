## Two systemic pains that came up outside the round log

1. **Hiders can't tell if they're inside the hiding zone.** No live-distance-to-centre readout — checking means switching to Google Maps and eyeballing, which nobody actually did during hiding time.
2. **Anchor-point sharing is manual and lossy.** Seekers dropped their live location, radar centres, and thermometer endpoints into WhatsApp. Hiders then had to find the pin, transcribe it into the app, and re-measure to answer — enough friction that it degrades every question involving a seeker-side point.

## What actually got used on the app

- Thermometer (1×), radar (2×), endgame — the three tools that take a single tap-and-place with no set-up.

## What got skipped despite being buildable

- Range-exclusion on a metro line (Q0)
- Whole-line exclusion (Q1)
- Name-length matching with cross-outs (Q5)
- POI-category measuring — parks (Q6), theatres (Q9)

The pattern: anything that needs the player to *express what they've ruled out* rather than *ask a fresh question* fell off the app.
